About England

England is a country that forms part of the United Kingdom, located on the island of Great Britain in the North Atlantic Ocean. It is bordered to the north by Scotland, to the west by Wales, and to the south by the English Channel, which separates it from continental Europe. England has a rich history and plays a significant role in world culture, politics, and economics.

Famous Landmarks

Big Ben and the Houses of Parliament – Iconic symbols of England.

British Museum – World-renowned museum housing millions of works of art and artifacts from around the world.

Hadrian’s Wall – Roman-built wall marking the northern boundary of the Roman Empire in Britain.

Tower Bridge – Famous bascule and suspension bridge across the River Thames.

Buckingham Palace – The official residence of the British monarch, known for its Changing of the Guard ceremony.

Stonehenge – A Prehistoric stone circle, a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Roman Baths – A major tourist attraction and part of the UNESCO World Heritage site of Bath built around natural hot springs.

Lake District – A national park famous for its stunning landscapes, lakes, and mountains, offering popular hiking and nature experiences.

Canterbury Cathedral – UNESCO World Heritage site, a key place of Christian pilgrimage.

Westminster Abbey – Historic church and site of royal coronations, weddings, and funerals, with graves of notable historical figures.

Economy and Government

The economy of the United Kingdom is a highly developed social market economy. It is the sixth-largest national economy in the world measured by nominal gross domestic product (GDP), tenth-largest by purchasing power parity (PPP), and twentieth by nominal GDP per capita, constituting 3.1% of nominal world GDP.

Britain's government system is a parliamentary democracy and constitutional monarchy. Parliamentary democracy means that U.K. nationals have the power to elect their legislative representatives, whose work is to present the citizen's interests to the government.

Sports

Sports hold a central place in England’s cultural identity, shaping its social fabric and bringing communities together. From historic traditions to modern-day competitiveness, sports are deeply embedded in the country's heritage, contributing to both national pride and international success. Football, cricket, rugby, tennis, and golf are not only enjoyed by millions but are also significant contributors to the nation's economy and global influence in sports.

Football (Soccer)

In England, football reigns supreme as the most popular sport, drawing massive crowds and broadcasting audiences, especially during major tournaments and league competitions.

Cricket

Cricket, often considered England’s national sport, carries centuries of tradition and has fostered international rivalries, making it a key part of England's sporting legacy.

Rugby

Rugby, with its passionate fanbase, is a key sport in both the union and league formats, regularly featuring England at the forefront of international competitions.

Tennis

Tennis, particularly through the iconic Wimbledon tournament, showcases England’s long-standing connection to the sport and its global stature.

Golf

Golf, with its rich history and renowned courses, continues to be a favorite pastime and a competitive sport, with events like The Open Championship drawing attention from around the world.

History and Culture

England, which had subsumed Wales in the 16th century under Henry VIII, united with Scotland in 1707 to form a new sovereign state called Great Britain. Following the Industrial Revolution, which started in England, Great Britain ruled a colonial Empire, the largest in recorded history.

England's rich customs and traditions are famous across the world. English culture frequently gets associated with copious amounts of tea drinking, the British Royal family and good manners. However, English culture goes far deeper than these internationally recognised hallmarks and is evolving all the time.
